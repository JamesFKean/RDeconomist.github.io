Coronavirus charts for the UK, updated daily.

Cases

The first chart is coronavirus cases for the English regions. The data plotted is the rolling 7-day sum. Highlight your own region by clicking on its name on the chart's legend.

The next chart shows new Covid cases reported each day. The regions are stacked on top of one another to give the national picture. Use the drop down box to select a particular region.

The third chart splits out the regions in a 'trellis' plot. This makes it easier to compare and contrast regions.

Deaths

The first chart shows deaths attributed to Covid across the four home nations. Note the differneces in scale on the vertical axis.

The relationship between cases and deaths is changing over time.

The crisis has had three distinct phases. The next chart shows this by plotting cases against deaths. In the first phase, the spring months, deaths rose and fell sharply with cases. In the second, deaths rose much more slowly. Most recently, the new UK variant has seen a jump in cases

Risks: cases and old age

The most at risk areas are those with a high numbers of cases, and high numbers of residents aged 75+. Hover over the dots to reveal the names of each of these local areas.
