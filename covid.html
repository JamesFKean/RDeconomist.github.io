A set of Covid-related charts for the UK. They run direct from official government data and update daily, so check back to see our progress against Covid-19.

The first chart shows new Covid cases reported each day in England since the start of the pandemic. The regions are stacked on top of one another to give the national picture. Use the drop down box to select a particular region.

The second chart splits out the regions in a 'trellis' plot. This makes it easier to compare and contrast regions.

The next chart shows new Covid cases, over a 14 day rolling window to smooth out the data a little. Hovering over the data with a mouse will give you both the number of cases plotted and the raw data, i.e. the acutal number of cases that day.

Covid cases by region - past 30 days

The pattern between regions is similar, but in recent weeks some differences have appeared. The chart below looks at the last month's data. Cases have started to rise again in London.

Cases and deaths

The relationship between cases and deaths is changing over time.

Two risks: cases and age

The most at risk areas are those with a high numbers of cases, and high numbers of 75+ residents. Hover over the dots ont the chart to releval the names of each of these local areas.
