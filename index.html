This is a personal test site I use to try out new charting code. The aim is to have interactive charts, built on open-source code, that are downloadable and verifiable. The Covid-19 pandemic has produced a torrent of data, but with some notable exceptions, the graphical analysis of it has been poor. Mainly, the problem is timing: most of what we see in the news is days or weeks out of date, resting on technology used since early 1980s. In addition there has been false information.

The charts here are all live, either running direct from the original data sources, or are updated daily via a script written by me. An example below tracks Covid-19 cases across the UK regions. Try clicking on the legend to highligh a region of interest, and hover over the lines with your mouse to see the data.

The charts are drawn in Vega-Lite, which I have put together based on excellent tutorials from the University of Washington and Observable. You can find this project on GitHub page, contact details are below. | Richard Davies
